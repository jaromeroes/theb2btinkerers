01 Context & Origin

The B2B Tinkerers was born from a personal and professional reality. After 12+ years leading marketing teams in B2B industrial and technology companies — including THIELMANN and Mimacom/Flowable — the founder identified a persistent gap in the market.

01
Missing strategic leadership

Most B2B companies of medium size have marketing teams but no strategic leadership. Activities happen, but without direction.

02
Agency vs. insider perspective

External agencies bring an agency perspective, not a business one. They optimise for deliverables, not for outcomes.

03
Activity without impact

Marketing activity rarely connects to business outcomes. Campaigns run, budgets are spent, but the bridge to revenue is missing.

'Tinkerers' is plural by design — the vision is a firm where multiple senior practitioners, each with deep insider experience, serve clients across markets. The founder is the first Tinkerer, but not the last.

02 Who We Serve

The common thread is not company size — it is the situation. We work with CEOs and founders of B2B tech and industrial companies where marketing has no strategic direction.

The client

CEO or founder of a B2B tech or industrial company. Has a marketing team but no real strategic direction. Marketing executes but doesn't decide.

The situation

Marketing spends budget but the CEO can't tell if it's working. No clear connection between what marketing does and what the business needs.

Who we are NOT for
✕

Companies looking for a traditional agency to produce content or run campaigns.

✕

Junior marketing managers seeking tactical support — they may see us as competition, not help.

✕

Companies that want more marketing activity. We are for companies that want better marketing outcomes.

03 The Problem We Solve

The real problem is not a lack of marketing talent or budget. It is a lack of alignment between marketing and business.

Core insight

Marketing teams measure activity. CEOs need to see impact on the business. Nobody has built the bridge between the two — and that gap is costing B2B companies real money.

01
Marketing and business are not aligned

Marketing has its own objectives. Sales has theirs. The CEO has the business goals. Nobody is working from the same page.

02
Activity is not the same as impact

Paid campaigns, events, content — all running. But when someone asks "is it working?", the honest answer is: we don't really know.

03
The brand doesn't reflect where you're going

The market has changed. The business has evolved. But the brand still says what it said three years ago.

04
The marketing plan exists. But nobody follows it

It's on a slide deck somewhere. It doesn't connect to real business objectives — so it gets ignored.

The twist

Your marketing team is not the problem. They have the skills. They have the energy. What they're missing is direction, alignment, and someone who has sat in that chair before.

07 Engagement Model

Depth first, then continuity. Three phases — each with a clear purpose and a natural transition to the next.

01
Audit & Diagnosis

Understand the current state: positioning, brand, activities, team, business objectives. The natural entry point — low risk for the client, high value from day one.

02
Strategic Build

Positioning, GTM strategy, marketing plan, AI & hybrid team setup. Designed for the client's team, budget, and reality — not for a presentation.

03
Advisory Retainer / Fractional CMO

Flexible ongoing engagement — from a light monthly advisory retainer to a more active Fractional CMO role. Senior marketing leadership without the full-time cost. A fraction of a CMO salary, the same strategic impact.

04
Project Execution

The audit surfaces gaps. Some of those gaps require execution — a rebranding, a new website, a platform migration, a campaign build. These are scoped and quoted separately as standalone projects, and appear in the client's roadmap as concrete next steps.

Execution is flexible by design: projects can be led directly with the support of trusted third parties, or we step in as project lead when the client already has their own supplier. The key is that strategic ownership stays consistent — the same person who defined what needs to happen also ensures it happens correctly.

On projects: A project only enters the roadmap if it was identified through the audit — not because it's a service we want to sell. This keeps the client's trust intact and the scope honest.

Martech Review

A dedicated service that sits across all phases of the engagement. Most B2B mid-market companies have accumulated tools over time — without a clear architecture, without full adoption, and often paying for platforms nobody uses. The review brings clarity and ROI back to the stack.

01
Stack audit

Map every tool in use: what it does, who uses it, how much it costs, and whether it's actually being used. Most companies are surprised by what they find.

02
Tool recommendation

Based on the team's size, maturity, and objectives — not on vendor relationships. CRM, marketing automation, analytics, SEO, ABM, and AI tooling assessed against what the company actually needs and can realistically operate.

03
Implementation & migration

From selection to go-live. Managed directly or in coordination with the client's existing IT or agency resources. Configuration, data migration, integrations, and QA.

04
Team enablement

A tool nobody knows how to use is a tool nobody uses. Training is built into every implementation — tailored to the team's role, not a generic vendor tutorial.

On Martech: The goal is never the most sophisticated stack. The goal is the most effective stack for that specific team. Simpler, well-adopted, and properly integrated beats complex and underused every time.

On the retainer: The goal is not to create dependency. The retainer exists to maintain direction, catch drift early, and provide a senior voice when decisions matter.

Roadmap
01
Visual direction · Logo · Design system

Choose the visual direction from the moodboard exploration, design the logo, and build the full design system. The visual foundation that everything else depends on.

02
Tagline

A single line that crystallises the positioning. Needs to be resolved before the web is built — it shapes how the brand is introduced everywhere.

03
Content pillars

Define the 3–5 themes the brand will own. Structures the web copy, the thought leadership, and the editorial calendar before any page is written.

04
Website

With visual direction, tagline, and pillars in place, the web can be built with real clarity. Not a placeholder — a conversion tool.

05
LinkedIn voice guide

Define how the brand shows up on LinkedIn — content formats, posting cadence, tone rules, and what to never say. The web becomes the reference point.

06
Proposal · Deck template

Once the brand has a visual system and a clear voice, the commercial documents build themselves. The deck is the final expression of everything above.
